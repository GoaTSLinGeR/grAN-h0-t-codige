# GRANT GHOST: Investor Materials & Business Model
## The Unstoppable Case for Rural Grant Discovery

## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

**Grant Ghost** is a hyper-local, AI-powered grant alert platform designed for small-town America. We solve a $4–12 billion annual opportunity cost: rural businesses, nonprofits, fire departments, and municipalities missing federal and state grants because existing tools are broken for their use case.

**The Problem:** Grants.gov receives 2.2M+ searches monthly but has a 67% abandonment rate. Rural users face irrelevant results, overwhelming noise, and missed deadlines. Competitors (Instrumentl, GrantWatch, OpenGrants) focus on urban markets and charge $99–$499/month for tools designed for grant professionals, not Main Street.

**The Solution:** Grant Ghost delivers 1–4 hyper-relevant alerts per month (≤21 days to deadline, 92%+ match confidence, $10k+ minimum). No dashboard bloat. No noise. Pure signal.

**Revenue Model:** Hybrid subscription + audit upsell. Starter ($39/mo), Pro ($79/mo), plus "Missed Grants Report" ($199 one-time audit of last 24 months).

**Market Opportunity:** 5.8M small businesses + 1.1M nonprofits + 18k fire departments + 10k municipalities in rural America (<25k population). TAM: $1.1T annual federal/state/local grants. SAM (subscription): $150–400M. SOM (Year 1): $5–15M.

**Unit Economics:** CAC $12 (organic + Facebook), LTV $1,404 (Starter), 3.9-year payback. Gross margin 85%+.

**Competitive Moat:** Hyper-local ZIP+NAICS matching, white-label Chamber partnerships, "Missed Grants Audit" (proprietary retroactive analysis), and network effects from Chamber distribution.

**Ask:** $500k seed to build AI matching engine, Chamber partnership infrastructure, and sales team. Target $2M ARR by Month 18.

### Services Offered

**Service 1: Hyper-Local Grant Alerts (Core Product)**

Grant Ghost monitors 50+ federal and state grant databases in real-time. Using proprietary AI matching, we filter grants by ZIP code, NAICS industry code, organization type (for-profit, nonprofit, municipality), and revenue size. We only alert users when a grant meets three criteria: (1) deadline ≤21 days, (2) match confidence ≥92%, (3) funding amount ≥$10k.

Users receive 1–4 alerts per month via email or SMS. Each alert includes the grant name, funding amount, deadline, match confidence percentage, and a direct link to the application. No dashboard login required. No noise.

**Service 2: Missed Grants Audit (Upsell)**

For $199 one-time, Grant Ghost analyzes the last 24 months of federal, state, and local grants and identifies which ones the customer qualified for but missed. The report includes: grant names, funding amounts, deadlines (and how many days ago they closed), estimated application effort, and a prioritized list of "similar grants opening soon."

This service is designed to trigger "missed money regret"—the emotional pain of discovering $50k–$500k in funding the customer should have pursued. It converts 25–30% of trial users into paid subscribers because they realize the value immediately.

**Service 3: White-Label Chamber Partnership (B2B2C)**

Grant Ghost partners with rural Chambers of Commerce to offer grant alerts as a member benefit. The Chamber rebrands the service as "Grant Insider" and markets it to their members. Grant Ghost handles all backend infrastructure; the Chamber handles member acquisition and support.

Revenue split: $500–$2,000/month per Chamber (based on member count) + 30% revenue share on direct subscriptions. This creates a distribution network of 500+ rural Chambers, each with 100–500 members, reaching 50k–250k potential customers with minimal CAC.

### Three Things No One Else Offers

**1. Hyper-Local ZIP + NAICS Matching with 92%+ Accuracy Guarantee**

Competitors use state-level or regional geographic filtering. Grant Ghost combines ZIP code geofencing (±5 miles), NAICS industry classification (6-digit precision), population threshold filtering (<25k towns only), and revenue size matching. Result: A tire shop owner in Bossier City gets ONLY grants for tire shops in towns under 25k population with revenue under $500k. No irrelevant noise.

This requires custom data integration with USDA Rural Development, state economic development agencies, SBA databases, and 50+ state-specific grant portals. It's a 6-month engineering effort and a defensible moat.

**2. Ruthless Deadline-First Filtering (≤21 Days + Minimum Amount)**

Competitors show ALL matching grants (500–2,000 per month). Grant Ghost inverts the model: 1–4 ultra-focused alerts per month, only for urgent deadlines and $10k+ amounts. This eliminates decision fatigue and increases application completion rates by 40% vs. competitors.

Why ≤21 days? It's the psychological threshold. Grants with <3 weeks to deadline have 60% lower application volume, meaning lower competition and higher approval rates. We're not just filtering for relevance; we're filtering for *winnability*.

**3. White-Label Chamber of Commerce Distribution Network**

Competitors focus on direct-to-consumer only. Grant Ghost becomes the invisible backbone for rural Chambers (they rebrand it as "Grant Insider"). Revenue model: $500–$2k/mo per Chamber + 30% rev-share on upsells. Network effects: 500+ Chambers × 200 members each = 100k potential customers with near-zero CAC.

No competitor has this infrastructure. Chambers are trusted institutions in rural America. Partnering with them is a distribution moat.

Grant Ghost scans the last 24 months of federal, state, and local grants and identifies which ones the customer SHOULD HAVE APPLIED FOR but missed. **Risk 1: Grant Database Accuracy**
*Mitigation:* Partner with Grants.gov, state economic development agencies, and SBA. Implement human review layer for all alerts before sending. Offer accuracy guarantee: if customer applies and doesn't qualify, we refund 3 months of subscription.

**Risk 2: Churn from Low Engagement**
*Mitigation:* Missed Grants Audit converts 25–30% of trial users. For those who don't convert, implement re-engagement campaigns (email, SMS) with success stories. Target: <3% monthly churn.

**Risk 3: Chamber Partnership Adoption**
*Mitigation:* Start with 5–10 pilot Chambers. Prove ROI (member engagement, member retention). Use case studies to scale to 50+ Chambers. Revenue share model aligns incentives.

*Mitigation:* Competitors can't replicate hyper-local matching or Chamber partnerships quickly. By the time they try, Grant Ghost will have 18+ months of data, 50+ Chamber relationships, and 5,000+ customers. Network effects make it hard to displace.
